1. Executive Summary
BA-in-a-Box is a purpose-built Business Analysis operating system. This updated proposal
reflects the revised scope following client review, with five critical additions that sharpen the
product's differentiation and prevent it drifting into generic project management or decorative AI
territory.
The five additions incorporated into this version:
•​
•​
•​
•​
•​

A baselined ML engine seeded with BA industry playbooks, capable of learning from
users over time
A formally defined BA ontology - entities, relationships, and lifecycle states - giving the
product its 'brain'
A precisely defined minimum lovable BA workflow with an exact user journey from
initiative creation to outcome evaluation
A Decision Intelligence Layer adding root-cause analysis, KPI trend analysis, anomaly
detection, and conversational querying
An AI-guided Problem Discovery module as the mandatory entry point to every BA
initiative

The core product architecture is: BA-native workflow >> structured artefacts >> knowledge
ingestion >> AI-assisted analysis >> decision intelligence.

2. What Makes This Different
The risk with any BA tool is that it becomes another project management board with AI labels on
it. This product is architected to avoid that outcome at the foundation level, not as an
afterthought.

Not a PM Tool
•​

•​

PM tools manage tasks, timelines, and resources. BA-in-a-Box manages the intellectual
work of analysis - problem definition, stakeholder alignment, requirement integrity, and
decision quality.
BA workflow stages, artefacts, and governance exist because the BA discipline requires
them - not because they are generic features.

Not Decorative AI
•​
•​
•​

AI features are embedded in the BA ontology, not bolted on. Every AI output is traceable
to a specific artefact, relationship, or lifecycle state.
The baseline ML engine learns from real BA work, not just static prompts. It improves as
the platform is used.
The insight engine goes beyond summarisation into root-cause analysis, anomaly
detection, and conversational querying of live project data.

The Product Has a Brain
The BA ontology formally defines how business goals, requirements, decisions, risks, and
solution evaluations connect across time. Every relationship in the system is intentional, typed,
and queryable. This is the structural foundation that makes deep analytics possible.

periodically, updating the model without requiring manual intervention. Over time, the product
becomes measurably smarter on the specific types of initiatives the organisation runs.
